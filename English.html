Foundation

Note-Making Fundamentals

Master the core principles of effective English literature note-taking.

Why Effective Notes Matter

  • Better Retention: Writing helps encode information in long-term memory
  • Quick Revision: Condensed notes save time during exam preparation
  • Understanding Connections: Notes help you see relationships between themes and ideas
  • Exam Confidence: Well-organized notes boost confidence and performance
Retention Revision Confidence

Core Principles

  • Clarity: Use clear headings, bullet points, and structured layouts
  • Conciseness: Capture key ideas in your own words; summarise instead of copying
  • Connections: Link themes, characters, and literary devices
  • Consistency: Develop a consistent format and stick to it
Clarity Conciseness Consistency

Tools & Setup

Physical: Quality notebooks, colored pens (blue/black/red), highlighters, sticky notes

Digital: Notion or OneNote for organised digital notes, Google Docs for collaboration, Canva for mind maps, Grammarly for writing assistance

Notebook Digital Tools

Pro Tip — Start Simple

Don't try to use every technique at once. Start with one method, master it, then gradually add others. The best note-taking system is the one you'll actually use consistently.

Character Profile Method

Best for: In-depth character study

  • Physical description + personality traits
  • Motivations and relationships
  • Character arc across the story
  • Key dialogue that reveals character

Theme Identification

Best for: Essay preparation

  • Ask: what is the author saying about life?
  • Track repeated words, symbols, situations

Pro Tip — Combine Methods

Use annotations while reading, theme notes during revision, and character maps before writing essays. The most effective students never rely on just one method.

Examples

Sample Notes

Real-world examples of organised English literature notes using these techniques.

"Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?" — William Shakespeare

Form & Structure

  • Type: Shakespearean sonnet
  • Rhyme scheme: ABAB CDCD EFEF GG
  • Meter: Iambic pentameter
  • Structure: 3 quatrains + 1 couplet

Theme

Eternal beauty through poetry

Key Literary Devices

  • Metaphor: Comparing beloved to summer's day
  • Personification: "rough winds do shake"
  • Paradox: Eternal life through mortal verse

Analysis

  • Lines 1-8: Summer is beautiful but imperfect
  • Lines 9-12: Beloved's beauty is eternal
  • Lines 13-14: Poetry grants immortality

Key Quote

"So long as men can breathe or eyes can see, / So long lives this, and this gives life to thee"

"The Luncheon" — W. Somerset Maugham

Setting

  • Time: Flashback to past; narrated 20 years later
  • Place: Foyot's restaurant, Paris
  • Atmosphere: Elegant, expensive

Characters

  • Narrator: Young, struggling writer (broke)
  • Woman: Middle-aged admirer (manipulative, pretentious)

Themes

Exploitation of kindness · Social pretension · Poetic justice

Literary Techniques

  • Irony: Woman becomes very fat (justice)
  • Humor: Self-deprecating narrator
  • Contrast: Past struggle vs. present success

Note-Making in Action

Step 1: First reading (no notes — understand the story) → Step 2: Active reading (highlight quotes, devices) → Step 3: Note creation (use templates, connect ideas) → Step 4: Review & refine (add connections between themes)

Revision

Effective Revision Strategies

Systematic approaches to consolidate your English literature knowledge and perform your best on exam day.

Revision Timeline
  • Day 1: Take detailed notes
  • Day 3: Quick review (10 minutes)
  • Day 7: Comprehensive review (20 minutes)
  • Day 21: Test yourself (30 minutes)
  • Day 50: Final review before exam
Why spaced repetition works

Prevents the forgetting curve, strengthens neural pathways, improves long-term retention, and builds exam confidence.

Self-Testing
  • Cover notes, try to recall
  • Create flashcards for quotes
  • Write themes from memory
Study Groups
  • Discuss themes together
  • Quiz each other
  • Share different interpretations
Teaching Method
  • Explain to family/friends
  • Create presentations
  • Make video summaries

One Week Before

  • Review all major themes
  • Memorize key quotes
  • Practice essay outlines
  • Identify knowledge gaps
  • Create quick reference sheets

Day Before Exam

  • Light review only
  • Read key quotes once
  • Get proper sleep
  • Stay calm and confident
